About me

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Oslo, studying tumor heterogeneity in breast cancer through the reconstruction and comparison of single sample regulatory networks. My work has led to the development of the SiSaNA pipeline (https://github.com/kuijjerlab/sisana), which is software that helps users reconstruct single sample regulatory networks without the need of prior programming experience. During my PhD at Oregon State University, I studied the interplay between the gut microbiome and diseases (especially cancer and metabolic disease) of the host. Through the use of systems biology and network biology, I identified key factors (genes, microbiota, etc.) that are vital in the response to a treatment.
